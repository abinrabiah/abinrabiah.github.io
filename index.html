Ahmad Bin Rabiah

I'm a 1st year PhD student in Computer Science at UCSD, where I am fortunate to be advised by Professor Julian McAuley. My research interests lie in the intersection of machine learning and personalization. More specifically, I've been exploring problems related to recommender systems and conversational agents. My primary goal is to evolve the capacity of machines to perceive, understand, and personalize recommendations in a more sophisticated manner.

I completed my Master's in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Purdue University — Hail Purdue! It was during this time, in collaboration with Professor Qi Guo, where we worked on computer vision problem in which improving image reconstruction in multi-camera systems [arXiv '22].

My early experience traces back to my time at PSDSARC—an applied research center, where I contributed to developing signal processing systems. This was built upon my undergraduate years at KSU, where I worked with Professors Saleh Alshebeili and Omar Aldayel, working on signal processing and convex optimization [RadarConf '20].
